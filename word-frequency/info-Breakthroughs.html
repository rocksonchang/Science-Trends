Major Breakthroughs

Experimental quantum teleportation
Bouwmeester et al, Nature 390, 575-579 (1997)
The first experimental demonstration of the teleportation of a quantum state. This work represents a foundational building block in the field of quantum information processing.

Quantum phase transition from a superfluid to a Mott insulator in a gas of ultracold atoms
Greiner et al, Nature 415, 39-44 (2002)
The group at the MPQ demonstrated the first quantum phase transition using an ultracold gas, a major step forward for quantum simulation of complex many-body systems.

Synthetic magnetic fields for ultracold neutral atoms
Y.-J Lin et al, Nature 462, 628-632 (2009)
Ultracold gases are a promising platform for quantum simulation of many-body phenomena. Yet, they are limited since these gases are composed of neutral particles, whereas many condensed matter systems of interest are composed of charged particles. In 2009, The team at the JQI demonstrated a technique for the simulation of charged particles in a magnetic field using ultracold gases, thus opening up a new possibilities for quantum simulation.
